About Me

Biography

I am currently pursuing my PhD in Chemistry at the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Bombay, where I work in the field of computational chemistry, molecular magnetism, machine learning, and generative artificial intelligence.

My research focuses on understanding and designing molecular magnetic materials using advanced quantum chemical calculations, electronic structure methods, graph neural networks, and AI-driven inverse molecular design strategies.

I am particularly interested in lanthanide and transition-metal single-molecule magnets, magnetic anisotropy, zero-field splitting, spin dynamics, and structure–property relationships governing molecular magnetic behavior.

Research Supervisor

I am working under the supervision of Prof. Gopalan Rajaraman at the Department of Chemistry, Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Bombay.

His research focuses on molecular magnetism, quantum chemistry, spin crossover systems, electronic structure theory, and magnetic anisotropy in transition-metal and lanthanide complexes.
