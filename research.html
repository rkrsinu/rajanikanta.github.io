Research

Research Overview

My research focuses on the intersection of computational chemistry, molecular magnetism, and artificial intelligence. I am particularly interested in understanding and designing molecular magnetic materials using advanced quantum chemical calculations, machine learning techniques, and generative AI approaches.


My work primarily explores the electronic structure, magnetic anisotropy, spin dynamics, and relaxation mechanisms in transition metal and lanthanide-based molecular systems. By combining ab initio calculations, ligand-field analysis, and data-driven approaches, I aim to accelerate the discovery of next-generation single-molecule magnets and quantum magnetic materials.

Molecular Magnetism & Single-Molecule Magnets

A major part of my research is devoted to understanding the magnetic behavior of lanthanide and transition-metal complexes, particularly single-molecule magnets (SMMs). My studies involve detailed investigations of zero-field splitting, magnetic anisotropy, spin relaxation pathways, and magnetic exchange interactions using multireference quantum chemical methods.


I work extensively on Dy(III), Co(II), and heterometallic systems with various coordination geometries such as D5h, D6h, pseudo-linear, tetrahedral, and octahedral environments. The primary objective is to identify structure-property relationships that govern high magnetic blocking barriers and slow magnetic relaxation.

Machine Learning & Generative AI in Chemistry

I am actively developing machine-learning and generative-AI approaches for inverse molecular design in molecular magnetism. My work includes the development of graph neural network (GNN) models for predicting magnetic properties directly from ligand structures and coordination environments.


I am also exploring generative frameworks such as variational autoencoders (VAEs), genetic algorithms, and memory-augmented molecular design strategies for generating molecular systems with targeted magnetic properties, particularly desired zero-field splitting values and optimized anisotropy.

Quantum Chemical Calculations

My computational work extensively utilizes density functional theory (DFT), CASSCF, NEVPT2, ligand-field analysis, and ab initio spin-Hamiltonian approaches for understanding electronic structures and magnetic behavior.


I employ computational tools such as ORCA, OpenMolcas, Gaussian, and custom Python workflows to investigate magnetic anisotropy, exchange coupling, spin-phonon interactions, and excited-state electronic structures in complex molecular systems.

Hybrid Metal Halides & Photophysical Materials

In addition to molecular magnetism, my research also includes hybrid metal halides and low-dimensional luminescent materials. I investigate the role of lone-pair stereoactivity, structural distortion, hydrogen bonding, and dimensionality in controlling the optical and emission properties of Bi/Sb-based hybrid systems.


These studies combine crystal-structure analysis, spectroscopy, and computational investigations to understand the fundamental mechanisms governing photoluminescence and structure-property relationships in emerging functional materials.
